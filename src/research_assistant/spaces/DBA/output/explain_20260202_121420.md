> # Digital Resource Orchestration and Capital Efficiency in SaaS Management

## 1. Conceptual Definition

Based on the DRO Thesis Reference materials, Digital Resource Orchestration (DRO) refers to the internal governance mechanisms—specifically SaaS rationalization and coordinated management of digital assets—that organizations deploy to mitigate capital inefficiency.

Capital Efficiency in this context is measured through the lens of ROIC (Return on Invested Capital), examining how operational discipline in managing digital resources translates to superior financial performance.

The core problem your thesis addresses: Modern tech enterprises systematically waste 20–50% of their SaaS budgets through "zombie seats"—unused licenses caused by employee turnover and poor governance. This capital destruction is largely invisible to boards and investors yet represents a measurable drag on firm efficiency.
